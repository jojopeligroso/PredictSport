Rules

Everything you need to know.

A prediction game built as an homage to the World Cup's own format — groups, knockouts, and a final.

How it works

1
Pick the outcome of every match
Win, draw, or loss — one prediction per game, every day of the tournament.
2
Earn points, survive eliminations
Correct picks score points. At each cut, the lowest scorers go home.
3
Last one standing wins
Two survivors reach the final. Whoever has the most points when the trophy is lifted wins the game.

Scoring

Outcome Points
Correct prediction +3
Correct draw +1
Wrong prediction 0
Max per match day varies by fixtures scheduled. No negative scoring.

Format

Survivor

Survivor-style elimination. Groups, knockouts, last one standing.

Every player starts in a group. At the end of the Group Stage, the bottom finisher in each group is eliminated. In the Knockout Rounds, a fixed portion of the field is cut after each round — mirroring the World Cup bracket itself. Points reset to zero at each new stage.

The format is designed to mirror the World Cup. There are 12 groups of 4 in the real tournament — your prediction groups follow the same structure. As the tournament narrows, so does your competition.

How the cut works
Ranks 1–3 in each group advance to the Round of 32.
If #3 is within 3 points of #2, they're on the bubble — one correct pick can save them.
Rank 4 in each group is eliminated. No exceptions.

Knockout Rounds

After groups, the 36 survivors (3 per group × 12 groups) enter a single ranked pool. The Round of 32 keeps the top 32. From there, each round halves the field until only two remain.

Final

The last two survivors play out the World Cup Final alongside the real match. Whoever accumulates the most points across all knockout rounds wins the competition. In the event of a tie, tiebreaker rules apply — see below.

Variant

Full Bracket

Predict every match in the tournament from day one. Points accumulate over the full 64 games.

No elimination — everyone plays all the way through. The player with the most total points after the final wins. Ideal for groups who want a lower-stakes game with full participation to the end.

Variant

KO Bracket

Picks begin at the Round of 16. Shorter, punchier — every match matters from the start.

The Group Stage is skipped entirely. All players make predictions from the Round of 16 onwards. Scoring and elimination rules stay the same — just a tighter window. Good for late joiners or groups who want a two-week sprint.

Picks

Your picks

🔒
Picks lock 10 min before first kickoff each day. Any unpicked matches on that day are scored as 0.

You can change your prediction for any match right up until it locks. Once the day's first fixture kicks off, all predictions for that day are frozen — no edits allowed.

Predictions are outcome only — win, draw, or loss. You don't predict scorelines. A correct draw is worth +1 instead of +3 to reflect the added difficulty of calling a stalemate.

Playing ahead? You can submit picks for future match days early. If you know you'll be offline on a match day, lock in your predictions in advance and the system will use them.

Tiebreakers

If it's level

Three-step chain — applied in order until one player separates.

1
Most correct predictions
Total number of right calls across the stage, not just points.
2
Fewest wrong predictions
The player who was wrong less often earns the edge.
3
Coin flip
Rare, but if all else is equal — we leave it to chance. Literally.
