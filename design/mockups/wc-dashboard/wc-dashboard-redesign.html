Design Rationale

Key decisions and changes from the current state.

1

Two distinct states, not one page

Visitor and enrolled states are now meaningfully different. Visitor gets a landing page with format explanation and CTA. Enrolled user gets a dashboard focused on action — progress, picks, position. No ambiguity about what the primary action is for each user.

2

Matchday cards are significantly bigger

Existing cards use text-base (16px) with minimal padding. New cards use Inter 800 at text-lg (18px) with more generous padding and a 6px city-colour left border. The OUTCOMES/SCORES stats are now centered labels with progress bars — more scannable at a glance, and the host-city colour adds WC atmosphere without being distracting.

3

Classification hierarchy is explicit

FORMAT gets an amber left border and surface background — it's the primary, most novel classification. OVERALL is a plain bordered card. BRACKET is dashed and muted (both visitor and enrolled states). This hierarchy was previously unclear — all three competed for equal attention. The scoring table is always visible rather than hidden inside a fold-out.

4

Progress card as the enrolled hero

The enrolled user's most important question is "what have I done and what's left?" The amber-bordered progress card answers this immediately with three stat rows and a direct CTA. Previously there was no equivalent — the bracket card held that visual weight.

5

Leaderboard peek drives curiosity

A small "3rd of 8" card creates competitive tension without requiring a full tab switch. The monospace number in large type is immediately legible on scroll. Clicking goes to the full table.

6

Social CTA is contextual, not persistent

The WhatsApp/Copy link card appears after picks are set — when it's actually relevant. The invite icon in the navbar (with amber dot for pending friends) handles the always-available case. The dashed border signals it's a secondary action, not a primary destination.

7

WC rainbow strip + host-city colours

The animated gradient strip at the top of every screen connects the app to the official WC 2026 brand palette without requiring any licensed assets. The per-matchday city colours (Atlanta cyan, Kansas City red, Miami pink, etc.) make rounds visually distinct and tie to real geography — adding texture that the current neutral cards lack.

8

Visitor navbar has no clutter

Visitor navbar: brand mark + "Sign in" only. No tab bar, no icons. The enrolled navbar adds invite + hamburger — icons that are only meaningful once you're in. This prevents overwhelming first-time visitors with navigation they can't use yet.
