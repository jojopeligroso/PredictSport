Design rationale

1. Rainbow gradient strip (navbar)

Replaces the solid #006847 green line. The WC 2026 branding is defined by its multi-color geometric curves — carrying a 5px gradient strip across the top instantly signals "this is the World Cup" without touching the sportspredict brand below it. The effect is: tournament dressing on top of the app's own identity. When the WC ends, this strip disappears and the platform reverts to its neutral chrome. Every future competition could have its own accent strip.

2. Tournament context strip

Below the nav, a thin bar shows: tournament name, countdown, and who's playing (avatar stack). This serves two purposes: (a) it keeps the WC atmosphere present without a full hero image on every page, and (b) the avatar stack creates social proof — "other people are here, this is a competition, not a solo exercise." Tapping the avatar stack could expand to a participant list or invite flow.

3. Social CTA card — "When to invite?"

The answer: after the bracket, before the matchdays. This is the slot between the bracket hero card and the matchday list. The user has just committed to 104 predictions. They've proven engagement. The natural emotion is: "I want someone to compete against."

The card tone adapts to journey stage (variants A-D above). Early users get a soft invite. Mid-journey users get a cheeky prod. Close to kickoff, urgency. Once the user has enough friends (or dismisses), it collapses to a minimal strip showing participant count + subtle invite link.

WhatsApp is primary — this is Ireland, this is friend groups. Copy-link is secondary. The pre-filled WhatsApp message could be: "I'm in for the World Cup predictions. Think you can beat me? [link]" — competitive, short, easy to forward.

4. Persistent invite icon (navbar)

A user-plus icon in the navbar with a subtle amber notification dot. Always accessible. Doesn't take up much space. Tapping opens a share sheet (WhatsApp, copy link, QR code). The notification dot can be removed once the user has invited at least one person or the competition has 8+ members.

5. What this does NOT change

  • Matchday card structure — it works, clear and functional
  • Bracket hero card — effective, proven
  • Nav link labels — clear and scannable
  • The sportspredict brand identity — the WC dressing sits on top of it
  • The cream/amber/ink palette — this is the app's identity

6. Future: post-completion celebration share

The existing MatchdayCompleteCelebration modal could add a share action: "Share your picks" button that generates a WhatsApp message like "Just finished my Group Matchday 1 predictions. 24/24. Come at me. [link]". This is a natural emotional peak — the user just achieved something and wants to flex. Not in this prototype but a natural next step.
